## The Top 5 for *you* specifically

Your six priorities reward **places that combine genuinely world-class riding with swimmable beaches, sun, and great food.** That tilts the ranking toward the Mediterranean, the islands, and Andalusia over green-but-grey northern Spain (which wins on roads + food but loses on sun + beach-swimming).

### 🥇 1. Costa Blanca (Jávea/Dénia) — *the best all-rounder, and the cheapest*
A **toll-free 4-hour drive** (your own bike, or rent locally from ~€20/day). The **Coll de Rates** is a world-class twisty pass 40 min inland; the coast has both jaw-dropping turquoise *calas* (Granadella, Moraig) **and** long sandy beaches; Dénia is a **UNESCO City of Gastronomy** (gamba roja, arroz a banda). Hits all six priorities at a 5, and you can do it for **under €400**. *If you only read one section, read this one.*

### 🥈 2. Mallorca — *the bucket-list ride, by plane*
~€90–150 flights, ~20/day. **Sa Calobra** (the Coll dels Reis "tie-knot" spiral) and the **120 km MA-10** through the Serra de Tramuntana are legendary motorcycling. Pair with Caribbean-white *calas* in the south and the Valldemossa–Deià–Sóller villages. *Rent a proper bike (A2/A licence), not a 125 scooter, to do the Tramuntana justice. Ride Formentor before 10am — motorbikes are banned 10:00–22:00 in summer 2026.*

### 🥉 3. Málaga + Ronda — *world-class roads, world-class food, easiest access*
The **AVE train gets you there in 2h20, city-center to city-center** for ~€25–45 each way — the most relaxed Friday escape on the list. The **A-397** behind Ronda ("365 curves") and the **A-369** through the pueblos blancos are elite riding; add the **Caminito del Rey** and espetos on the beach. Only weak card: Costa del Sol beaches are good-not-pristine (go to Nerja/Maro). *Check the A-397's status the week you go — it has had 2025–26 landslide closures.*

### 4. Cabo de Gata + Las Alpujarras — *the wild card (literally)*
The single best **sun + wild-beach + epic-mountain-road trifecta** in Spain: Europe's only desert, volcanic *calas* (Genoveses, Mónsul), and the highest paved roads on the continent (Veleta, Puerto de la Ragua). Almería is the **sunniest, driest province in Europe.** The catch is the **~5.5h drive** — best as a long weekend, or fly into Almería/Granada.

### 5. Cádiz / Costa de la Luz (Tarifa) — *elite beaches, food & nature; ride with Africa on the horizon*
Bolonia's dune + Roman ruins, the world's best **almadraba bluefin tuna** (El Campero), sherry in Jerez (2026 Gastronomic Capital of Spain), **orca-watching in July–August**, and the southernmost road in mainland Spain looking across to Morocco. Riding is good (Los Alcornocales twisties) rather than legendary, and on-coast bike rental is the weak point — but as an *experience* it's unmatched. Fly to Jerez (XRY).

## 🚗 By car from Madrid

### 1. Costa Blanca — Jávea (Xàbia) / Dénia / Alicante  ⭐ TOP PICK
**Drive:** ~420 km / **~4h** to Alicante, ~465 km / ~4.5h to Jávea-Dénia. The **A-31 is completely toll-free** (the Alicante bypass became permanently free in late 2025). Easy Friday-evening run: leave ~18:00, arrive Jávea ~23:00. *Base in **Jávea or Dénia** — best access to both coast and the inland mountain passes.*

**🏍️ Riding — genuinely world-class (5/5).**
- **Coll de Rates (CV-715):** from Parcent, ~6.5 km of immaculate switchbacks to the 628 m pass, then a technical descent to Tárbena. The regional benchmark road.
- **Vall de Guadalest loop:** Coll de Rates → Tárbena → the cliff-top castle village of **Guadalest** above its turquoise reservoir → Vall de Gallinera. A classic full day.
- **Port de Tudons / Puerto de Confrides:** longer serious mountain climbs.
- **Coastal balcony roads (CV-740/742)** out to **Cabo de la Nao** and the Jávea miradores.
- **Rental:** Moto Llopis (Dénia) from ~€20/day; Alicante has deep supply (scooters €10–25, touring/BMW-class €60–120/day).

**🏖️ Beaches (5/5) — rare combo of dramatic coves *and* sand.**
- **Cala de la Granadella** (Jávea): turquoise pebble cove ringed by pine cliffs — routinely voted one of Spain's best.
- **Cala del Moraig** (Benitàchell): limestone cliffs + the swim-in **Cova dels Arcs** sea cave.
- **Playa del Arenal** (Jávea): the lively golden-sand crescent.
- **Playa de Las Rotas** (Dénia): marine reserve, superb snorkelling.
- **Playa de la Fossa** (Calpe): sand beneath the towering **Peñón de Ifach**.

**🍴 Food (5/5).** **Gamba roja de Dénia** (red prawn, served simply boiled), **arroz a banda**, **fideuà**, **caldero**. Eat at **El Pegolí** or **Casa Federico** (Dénia, seafront prawns + rice), and **Nou Manolín** or **Piripi** (Alicante — Michelin-selected rice houses).

**🌿 Nature & exploring (4.5/5).** Hike the **Peñón de Ifach** (332 m sea-monolith, 300 people/day cap), **Montgó Natural Park**, **Cabo de la Nao** lighthouse + the 15 cliff *miradores*, **Guadalest** village, and swim in the **Fonts de l'Algar** waterfalls. Day-trip the white town of **Altea**.

**📅 2-day plan.** *Sat:* bike day — Parcent → Coll de Rates → Tárbena → Guadalest → swim at Algar falls → coastal miradores → sunset at Granadella → seafood dinner in Dénia. *Sun:* dawn hike up the Peñón de Ifach → beach at La Fossa → lunch at Nou Manolín/Piripi in Alicante → drive home (~21:00).

**💶 Budget (1 person, 2 nights):** ~**€300–400** budget / €550–700 mid-range. Toll-free, cheap rental = the best value on this list.

**Verdict:** *Everything you want, on a free 4-hour drive — world-class twisties, turquoise coves and sandy beaches, elite seafood, and dramatic rock-and-sea nature.*

### 10. Murcia / Costa Cálida — Cartagena, Mar Menor, Calblanque
**Drive:** ~444 km / **~4h10**, A-3 → A-31 → A-30, **toll-free**. Genuine Friday-evening 2-night weekend (arrive ~22:30 for late tapas).

**🏍️ Riding (4/5).** **Sierra Espuña** (tight pine-forest hairpins around Collado Bermejo / RM-503), the **Sierra de Cartagena coastal loop** to the clifftop **Batería de Castillitos** gun battery, and the **Mazarrón coast** to Águilas. Rentals: Momoven Cartagena from ~€25/day.

**🏖️ Beaches (4/5).** **Calblanque** — a wild protected park of golden dunes and turquoise water, no development (summer car ban 8:00–20:30; park + shuttle ~€3). Plus the warm, shallow, family-friendly **Mar Menor** lagoon (Santiago de la Ribera, Lo Pagán) and diving at **Cabo de Palos**.

**🍴 Food (4/5).** **Caldero del Mar Menor** (cauldron rice with rockfish broth, served in courses) at **Los Calderos de Julia** (Santiago de la Ribera); **marinera** (anchovy-topped ensaladilla ring) and **michirones** at **El Churra** (Murcia city).

**🌿 Nature (4/5).** Roman **Cartagena** (1st-c. BC theatre, naval history), **Sierra Espuña**, the **San Pedro del Pinatar** salt flats (flamingos late June + Lo Pagán mud baths).

**📅 2-day plan.** *Sat:* Cartagena old town + Roman theatre → Sierra de Cartagena ride to Castillitos → wild swim at Calblanque → caldero dinner on the Mar Menor. *Sun:* San Pedro salt flats / flamingos / mud baths → drive home. *(Moto variant: dedicate Saturday to Sierra Espuña.)*

**💶 Budget:** ~**€220–320 pp** (no bike). The cheapest weekend here, and the easiest Friday departure.

**Verdict:** *The doable, food-and-variety weekend — city culture + warm lagoon + one wild beach + good mountain riding, all in a tidy 4 hours.*

### 4. Cabo de Gata + Sierra Nevada / Las Alpujarras (Almería)
**Drive:** ~560–600 km / **~5h30** (A-4 → A-44 → A-92), toll-free and scenic but long — **best as a long weekend, or fly to Almería (LEI, ~1h15, €90–180 rt) or Granada (GRX)** for the mountain side. *Honest note: a strict Fri-night→Sun loses a near-full day to driving.*

**🏍️ Riding (5/5) — the strongest reason to come.** A ~250 km Sierra Nevada loop:
- **Puerto de la Ragua (A-337, ~2,000 m):** a glorious sweeping pass linking the Alpujarras to Guadix past **La Calahorra** castle.
- **Alto del Veleta (A-395):** ride extremely high toward Europe's highest road point (final park section is permit/minibus only).
- **Las Alpujarras serpentines (A-4132 / A-4129):** through the **Poqueira valley** villages **Pampaneira → Bubión → Capileira** and up to **Trevélez** (highest village in mainland Spain).
- **Cabo de Gata volcanic coast roads** around San José and the lighthouse.
- Rental in **Granada** (BMW GS, Africa Twin, Multistrada ~€67–130/day).

**🏖️ Beaches (5/5) — Spain's wildest Mediterranean coast.** **Playa de los Genoveses** (virgin golden bay), **Playa de Mónsul** (the *Indiana Jones* volcanic rock), **Playa de los Muertos** (crystal water), **Cala de San Pedro** (boat/hike-only hippie cove), **Agua Amarga** (chic fishing village). *Summer car access to Genoveses/Mónsul regulated 20 Jun–27 Sep — shuttle from San José ~€1–3.*

**🍴 Food (4.5/5).** Almería has the **free-tapa culture** (a real tapa with every drink) — bar-hop at **Casa Puga** (since 1870). Eat **gambas rojas de Garrucha**, **jamón de Trevélez**, and **plato alpujarreño** up in the mountains.

**🌿 Nature (5/5).** Cabo de Gata-Níjar park, **Faro de Cabo de Gata** + Arrecife de las Sirenas, flamingo salt flats, **Sierra Nevada NP** (Mulhacén 3,479 m), the Alpujarras white villages, and the **Tabernas Desert** — Europe's only desert, where the Spaghetti Westerns and *Lawrence of Arabia* were filmed (visit Fort Bravo / Mini Hollywood).

**💶 Budget:** ~**€540 budget / €990 comfortable** (with a weekend bike rental).

**Verdict:** *Europe's only desert, Spain's wildest volcanic beaches, and the highest motorcycle roads on the continent — for a rider it beats Málaga, if you can swallow the drive.*

## 🚄 By AVE high-speed train

### 3. Málaga + Ronda (Costa del Sol)
**Get there:** the **AVE/Avlo/iryo/OUIGO from Atocha in ~2h20**, ~20/day, **€25–45 each way** booked ahead — city-center to city-center, the most relaxed option on this whole list. (Flights MAD→AGP are ~1h15 / €75–120 but lose the time advantage door-to-door. Driving is ~5h15.) Rent the bike in Málaga and you don't need a car.

**🏍️ Riding (5/5) — Málaga's standout asset.**
- **A-397, San Pedro de Alcántara → Ronda:** Spain's most famous biking road, ~45 km and a reputed **365 curves** climbing to ~1,000 m. ⚠️ **2026 caveat: recurring landslide closures (Storm Jana 2025, fresh March-2026 slips, since reported reopened) — check status the week you travel;** the A-376/A-366 into Ronda are great fallbacks.
- **A-369, Ronda → Gaucín:** a ridge road through the pueblos blancos with views to Gibraltar and Africa.
- **Sierra de las Nieves National Park** roads (designated 2021; pinsapo firs, peaks near 2,000 m).
- Rental: **Hertz Ride** (from ~€67/day), **IMTBike**, **Hispania Tours** — a BMW R 1250/1300 GS ~€110–160/day.

**🏖️ Beaches (3/5 — honest).** Costa del Sol beaches are good-not-pristine (grey sand, packed in August). Best is **Nerja — Burriana & Maro** (cleaner, cliff-backed coves, snorkelling), 50 min east. La Malagueta is fine for a city swim.

**🍴 Food (5/5).** **Espeto de sardinas** (sardines grilled on a cane over beach embers — the signature experience, at a Pedregalejo/El Palo chiringuito), **pescaíto frito**, **Málaga sweet wine**, **El Pimpi** in the old town, and **rabo de toro** (oxtail) up in Ronda.

**🌿 Nature (4.5/5).** **Ronda** (the El Tajo gorge + Puente Nuevo), the **Caminito del Rey** cliff boardwalk (**book ahead — sells out weeks early; €10 GA / €18 guided**), **Cueva de Nerja**, white villages (Frigiliana, Mijas, Gaucín), dolphin trips from Nerja.

**📅 2-day plan.** *Fri PM:* AVE down, dinner at El Pimpi. *Sat (ride day):* GS up the A-397 to Ronda (status permitting) → Puente Nuevo + rabo de toro → A-369 loop through the pueblos blancos → back via A-366 → espetos at a chiringuito. *Sun:* pre-booked **Caminito del Rey** → swim + pescaíto at Nerja → late AVE home.

**💶 Budget:** ~**€515–790 pp** mid-range.

**Verdict:** *Arguably the single best weekend motorcycle escape from Madrid — legendary roads, 2h20 train access, sun and superb food — just book Caminito early and check the A-397's status.*

## ✈️ By plane — Balearic Islands

*All ~1h20 flights. **Mallorca** is cheapest/most frequent (~20/day, €90–200 rt), **Ibiza** mid (~8/day), **Menorca** hardest (~3/day, summer-seasonal). Sun is essentially guaranteed in summer (sea ~25–26°C); Menorca is the windiest (the Tramontana). **Book flights, cars and bikes months ahead** — summer demand spikes prices 30–60%. Eco-tax ~€3–4 pppn for mid-range hotels.*

### 2. Mallorca ⭐ the riding island
**🏍️ Riding (5/5) — world-famous.**
- **Sa Calobra (MA-2141 / Coll dels Reis, 682 m):** the bucket-list descent — ~26 hairpins and the **"Nus de sa Corbata,"** a 270° spiral where the road loops over itself; designed in 1933 "as close to a perfect motorsport road as possible." Ride it at dawn before the tour buses.
- **MA-10 (Serra de Tramuntana):** ~120 km of near-continuous curves past **Puig Major** (1,445 m), Andratx → Pollença. Worth two days.
- **Coll de Sóller old road (MA-11A):** 50+ hairpins, near traffic-free since the tunnel opened.
- **Coast road** through **Estellencs, Banyalbufar, Valldemossa, Deià.**
- **Rental:** 125 scooter €25–40/day; mid bike (Ducati Scrambler) ~€70–90; **adventure/touring (BMW GS, Triumph Tiger) ~€90–150+/day. Rent a real bike, not a 125** — the Tramuntana overwhelms a scooter. Needs A2/A licence for >125cc.
- ⚠️ **Cap de Formentor: motorcycles are BANNED 10:00–22:00, 15 May–18 Oct 2026** (camera-enforced, €100–200 fines). Ride it before 10am — also the best light at the miradors.

**🏖️ Beaches (4.5/5).** South: **Caló des Moro**, **Es Trenc** ("Caribbean"), **Cala Llombards**, **Cala Mondragó** (natural park). North/mountain: **Sa Calobra / Torrent de Pareis**, **Playa de Formentor** (shuttle-only in summer), **Playa de Muro** (family). *Several hotspots now have car bans — go early or use buses.*

**🍴 Food (4/5).** **Sobrassada, ensaïmada, tumbet, pa amb oli, arròs brut.** Traditional *cellers* — **Celler Sa Premsa** (Palma), **Can Ripoll** (Inca). Sóller prawns at **Es Fanals**. Fine dining: **Voro** (2★), **DINS Santi Taura**, **Béns d'Avall** (Sóller).

**🌿 Nature (5/5).** Tramuntana villages (**Valldemossa**'s Charterhouse where Chopin wintered, **Deià**, **Fornalutx**); the historic **1912 Sóller wooden train + 1913 tram** (combined return €40); **Coves del Drac** (underground lake + live concert); **Cap de Formentor** lighthouse; the **Sa Foradada** hike to the holed rock.

**📅 2-day plan.** *Sat:* dawn ride — Palma → Coll de Sóller old road → Sóller → MA-10 past Puig Major → **Sa Calobra** detour → lunch in Deià → Valldemossa → coast home. *Sun:* dawn run to **Cap de Formentor** (before 10am) → swim at a southern *cala* (Es Trenc / Mondragó) → Palma old town + ensaïmada → flight.

**💶 Budget:** ~**€450–700 pp** (cheapest island; ~€350 Palma-only on public transport).

**Verdict:** *The best motorcycle-plus-beach combo you can fly to in an hour — Sa Calobra alone justifies the trip.*

### 12. Menorca — the beach island (not the bike one)
**🏖️ Beaches (5/5) — the most pristine calas in the Balearics:** **Cala Macarella & Macarelleta**, **Cala en Turqueta**, **Cala Mitjana**, **Cala Galdana**, the red-sand **Cala Pregonda**. *Summer car ban Jun–Sep on the headline calas — use the Ciutadella Beach Buses (lines 68/69).*
**🏍️ Riding (2.5/5):** flat and gentle — Menorca is for the **Camí de Cavalls (GR223)**, a 185 km *walking/cycling* coastal path, not for passes. **🍴 Food (4/5):** **caldereta de langosta** (spiny-lobster stew) in **Fornells** (Es Cranc, Sa Llagosta), **Mahón cheese**, Gin Xoriguer. **🌿 Nature (4.5/5):** UNESCO **Talayotic** megaliths (Naveta des Tudons), **Monte Toro** viewpoint, biosphere reserve.
**Verdict:** *Pick Menorca if the trip is fundamentally about the most beautiful coves in Spain — not if it's about riding.*

### 13. Ibiza — sun, sunsets & scene
**🏖️ Beaches (4.5/5):** turquoise west coast — **Cala Comte**, **Cala d'Hort** (facing the **Es Vedrà** islet), **Cala Salada**, **Ses Salines**. **🏍️ Riding (3/5):** scenic coast roads to Es Vedrà and the north (Benirràs, Sant Joan) — pleasant cruising, not epic; scooters everywhere (~€25/day). **🍴 Food (4/5):** **bullit de peix**, **sofrit pagès**, **flaó**; **Es Boldadó** (Es Vedrà views), **Can Pilot**; stars at **La Gaia**. **🌿 Nature (4/5):** **Dalt Vila** (UNESCO old town), **Ses Salines** flamingo park, **Formentera** day-trip (ferry ~30 min to Ses Illetes). *Most expensive island by far — a club night can push a weekend past €1,500.*
**Verdict:** *Best vibe, sunsets and turquoise water — weakest of the three for a dedicated rider.*

## ✈️ By plane — Andalusia

### 5. Cádiz / Costa de la Luz — Tarifa, Bolonia, Zahara
**Get there:** **fly MAD → Jerez (XRY) ~1h15, €90–150 rt** (Iberia/Vueling; *no Ryanair on this route*), then rent a car at the airport (~50 min to the beach towns). Málaga (AGP) has more/cheaper flights but a ~1h45 drive — worth it mainly for better **bike rental** (Hispania Tours/Hertz Ride). The drive from Madrid is ~7h — a non-starter for a weekend. *No AVE to the coast; you need a car on arrival.*

**🏖️ Beaches (5/5) — elite.** **Playa de Bolonia** (4 km of golden sand + a 30 m shifting **dune** + the **Roman ruins of Baelo Claudia** right on the sand), **Playa de los Lances** & **Valdevaqueros** (Tarifa's kite/windsurf beaches), **Zahara de los Atunes**, **El Palmar** (surf), **Cabo de Trafalgar** (best sunset).

**🏍️ Riding (4/5).** The **N-340 around Tarifa** — the southernmost road in mainland Spain, **with Africa on the horizon** (stop at the **Mirador del Estrecho**); the coast run to **Los Caños de Meca**; and the twisty **Los Alcornocales** natural-park roads (Alcalá de los Gazules → Puerto de Gáliz). On-coast rental is thin (EagleRider Jerez); Málaga is the real hub.

**🍴 Food (5/5) — a highlight of the whole list.** This is **almadraba bluefin tuna** country — wild tuna netted as they migrate the Strait. Eat the *ronqueo* cuts (ventresca, tarantelo) at **El Campero** (Barbate, the temple of tuna; reserve ahead) or its casual **Taberna de El Campero** in Zahara; plus **Zoko**, **Casa Juanito**. Then **sherry in Jerez** (2026 Gastronomic Capital of Spain) — **Tío Pepe / González Byass** and **Lustau** cellar tours (~€20–30).

**🌿 Nature (5/5).** Europe's kite/windsurf capital (300+ windy days); **whale & dolphin watching with FIRMM** — and **orcas appear July–August** chasing the tuna, so a summer weekend gives you a real shot. White villages **Vejer de la Frontera** and **Medina Sidonia**; the **Parque Natural del Estrecho** with Morocco just 15 km across the water.

**📅 2-day plan.** *Sat:* Bolonia (dune + Baelo Claudia + swim) → ride the N-340 to the Mirador del Estrecho → FIRMM whale/orca trip from Tarifa → tuna dinner at El Campero. *Sun:* Jerez sherry bodega → Vejer white village → sunset at Cabo de Trafalgar → fly home.

**💶 Budget:** ~**€570–950 pp** (car-based); +€150–260 if you add a Málaga bike rental.

**Verdict:** *One of the best weekends in Spain for this exact traveler — world-class beaches and wind, the country's finest wild tuna, sherry, orcas, and Africa on the horizon. Riding is good rather than legendary, and you'll want to sort the bike via Málaga.*

## ✈️/🚗 By plane/car — Green Spain (the North)

> **Read this first:** the north is **green Atlantic Spain** — arguably the best **roads + food + scenery** on the list, but **not** reliable sun-and-swim. San Sebastián is sunny ~34–37% of summer days, ~6–7h sun, showers on ~40–50% of days; sea ~18–21°C (wetsuit territory). **Come for riding, food and dramatic coast; treat a warm beach day as a bonus.** Fly (~1h) rather than drive — there's no fast AVE yet.

### 7. Asturias + Picos de Europa — *the rider's secret*
**Get there:** fly MAD → Asturias (OVD) ~1h, or drive ~5h. Base in **Cangas de Onís**.
**🏍️ Riding (5/5) — one of Europe's best-kept secrets.** The **Desfiladero de los Beyos (N-625)** — a deep, narrow river gorge with a rock-tunnel portal into the Picos; **Puerto del Pontón** and **Puerto de San Glorio** (~1,609 m, the bear statue); the **AS-114** through Cabrales blue-cheese country; the **Mirador del Fito** spur (AS-260). *Tarmac varies and livestock roam unfenced — ride accordingly.* ⚠️ **Lagos de Covadonga: motorbikes are NOT exempt from the summer ban (1 Jun–19 Oct 2026)** — ride up **before 08:00** (then descend any time) or take the **€9 bus** from Cangas. Rental: Momoven (Gijón, BMW from ~€50/day); **EagleRider has a depot in Carreña de Cabrales, right inside the Picos** (BMW GS ~€158–187/day).
**🏖️ Beaches (4.5/5):** astonishing for the north — **Playa del Silencio**, the inland tidal sinkhole **Playa de Gulpiyuri**, the beaches around **Llanes**. **🍴 Food (4.5/5):** **sidra** (cider poured from height) in an Oviedo/Gijón *sidrería*, **cachopo**, **fabada**, **Cabrales** cheese. **🌿 Nature (5/5):** the **Lagos de Covadonga** (Enol & Ercina) + basilica, **Cangas de Onís**, the fishing villages **Lastres** and **Llanes**, **Bulnes**.
**Verdict:** *The best pure-riding weekend on the list if you accept northern weather — gorges, passes and green drama, with a few of Spain's prettiest hidden beaches thrown in.*

### 8. Cantabria + Liébana / eastern Picos
**Get there:** fly MAD → Santander (SDR) ~1h10 (**often the cheapest flights on the list, €48–100 rt**); drive ~4h.
**🏍️ Riding (5/5):** the **Desfiladero de La Hermida (N-621)** — Spain's longest gorge, ~20 km of twisties; **Potes** as the riders' hub; **Puerto de San Glorio**; up to the **Fuente Dé** cable-car base. **🏖️ Beaches (4/5):** **Playa de Oyambre** (dunes + estuary), **Somo/Loredo** (surf, ferry from Santander), **Playa de los Locos** (Suances — strong currents). **🍴 Food (4/5):** **anchoas de Santoña**, **cocido montañés**, **sobaos** & **quesada pasiega**. **🌿 Nature (5/5):** medieval **Santillana del Mar**, **Altamira** cave-art museum, **Cabárceno** wildlife park, the **Fuente Dé** cable car (750 m up in minutes).
**Verdict:** *The best mountain-pass riding in the north on a budget — food a notch below Basque, coast more for surfing than sunbathing.*

### 9. San Sebastián / Donostia + Basque coast — *the food capital*
**Get there:** fly MAD → Bilbao (BIO) ~1h04 (€80–180 rt, then ~1h to Donostia) or San Sebastián (EAS) ~1h10.
**🍴 Food (5/5) — the reason to go.** One of the world's great eating cities: a **pintxos crawl** in the Parte Vieja — **Bar Néstor** (legendary txuleta + tomato salad, ~8 seats, queue early), **La Cuchara de San Telmo** (beef cheek, cochinillo), **Borda Berri** (squid-ink orzo), **Gandarias**. **Txuleta** + **txakoli**. World-class Michelin density: **Arzak**, **Akelarre**, **Mugaritz**, and **Elkano** in Getaria (grilled turbot, World's-50-Best #24). **🏖️ Beaches (5/5):** **La Concha** is one of Europe's great urban bays; **Zurriola** (surf), **Zarautz**. **🏍️ Riding (4/5):** the coastal corniche **San Sebastián → Getaria → Zumaia → Lekeitio** and the **BI-3101** to **Gaztelugatxe**; rent a touring bike in **Bilbao** (IMTBike; SS shops are scooter-only), ~€80–150/day. **🌿 Nature (4/5):** **San Juan de Gaztelugatxe** (the *GoT* islet, timed tickets), the **Flysch cliffs at Zumaia**, the **Guggenheim Bilbao**.
**Verdict:** *The strongest single weekend in the north — elite food, a world-class city beach, and lovely coastal riding — just don't bank on Mediterranean sun.*

## ✈️ By plane — Portugal

### 6. Lisbon + coast (Sintra-Cascais / Arrábida) — *best Portugal all-rounder*
**Get there:** the most frequent route on the list — **~1h25, €60–150 rt** (Iberia, TAP, easyJet, Ryanair; ~130 flights/week). Drive is ~6h.
**🏍️ Riding (5/5):** the **Serra de Sintra** forest road (Sintra → **Cabo da Roca**, the westernmost point of mainland Europe); the **Cascais → Guincho** clifftop coast; and the **N379-1 "Estrada de Escarpa"** in **Arrábida** — ~14 km of clifftop curves to ~500 m, among Portugal's most scenic roads. Rental in Lisbon: scooters €10–25, BMW GS ~€75–89/day.
**🏖️ Beaches (4/5):** the wild, hike-in **Praia da Ursa**, surf at **Guincho**, and the turquoise Arrábida coves (**Galapinhos**, **Portinho da Arrábida**); chic **Comporta** to the south.
**🍴 Food (5/5):** **Time Out Market** (Manteigaria pastéis de nata), **Cervejaria Ramiro** (prawns, percebes), **Pastéis de Belém**, bacalhau, cataplana.
**🌿 Nature (5/5):** Sintra's **Pena Palace** & **Quinta da Regaleira**, **Cabo da Roca**, the Arrábida park.
**📅 2-day plan.** *Sat (moto):* Sintra (Regaleira + Pena) → Serra de Sintra road → Cabo da Roca → Praia da Ursa → Guincho → Cascais sunset. *Sun:* N379-1 Arrábida ride → swim at Galapinhos → Setúbal seafood lunch → Time Out Market + Belém → flight.
**Verdict:** *The best balanced Portugal weekend — short frequent flights, epic day rides, palaces, wild beaches and Portugal's top food scene from one base.*

### 11. Algarve (Lagos / Sagres / Costa Vicentina)
**Get there:** **fly to Faro (FAO) ~1h25, €60–140 rt** (mainly Ryanair) — **never drive (~7h).** **🏖️ Beaches (5/5):** Portugal's finest — **Praia da Marinha**, **Praia do Camilo**, **Dona Ana**, the **Benagil** sea cave, wild **Arrifana** on the Costa Vicentina. **🏍️ Riding (4/5):** the wild SW **Costa Vicentina** — **N268** to **Vila do Bispo** and **Cabo de São Vicente** (SW tip of Europe; windswept, can be cool even in summer). **🍴 Food (4/5):** **cataplana de marisco**, ultra-fresh grilled Atlantic fish, percebes. **🌿 Nature (4/5):** Seven Hanging Valleys trail, Sagres fortress, Costa Vicentina park. **Verdict:** *Portugal's most beautiful beaches + most reliable sun — fly to Faro; slightly less riding variety than Lisbon.*

### 14. Porto / Douro / Gerês — *for the N222 pilgrimage*
**Get there:** fly to Porto (OPO) ~1h20 (€60–150 rt); the Douro is ~1h15 east. **🏍️ Riding (5/5):** the **N222, Peso da Régua → Pinhão** (~21 km, ~93 bends through the Douro vineyards — **named the world's best driving road**); plus **Peneda-Gerês** national-park switchbacks. Porto rental from ~€59/day. **🏖️ Beaches (2/5):** cold Atlantic Costa Verde — the weak card. **🍴 Food (4/5):** **francesinha**, **port wine** tastings in Pinhão/Gaia. **🌿 Nature (5/5):** the UNESCO **Douro valley**, **Gerês** waterfalls, historic Porto. **Verdict:** *Go for the N222 riding pilgrimage and Port wine — accept weak beaches and that Douro + Gerês + Porto is a stretch in two days.*

## Practical notes (2026)

**🏍️ Summer access restrictions that include motorcycles — plan around them:**
| Place | Restriction (2026) | Workaround |
|---|---|---|
| **Cap de Formentor** (Mallorca) | Cars **and motorbikes** banned 10:00–22:00, 15 May–18 Oct; €100–200 fines | Ride before 10:00 (best light anyway) |
| **Lagos de Covadonga** (Asturias) | Motorbikes **not exempt**, 1 Jun–19 Oct (except 25 Jul & 8 Sep) | Ride up before 08:00, descend any time; or €9 bus |
| **Cabo de Gata** (Genoveses/Mónsul) | Car access regulated 20 Jun–27 Sep | Shuttle from San José ~€1–3 |
| **Menorca** calas (Macarella/Turqueta) | Car ban Jun–Sep | Ciutadella Beach Buses 68/69 |
| **Calblanque** (Murcia) | Vehicle ban ~8:00–20:30 peak summer | Park + shuttle ~€3 |
| **A-397** (Ronda) | Recurring 2025–26 landslide closures | Check status that week; A-366/A-376 fallback |

**📋 Book ahead (summer = peak):** flights, hotels and **bike rentals** sell out and prices climb 30–60% — reserve 1–3 months out, especially for **Menorca flights**, **El Campero** (Cádiz tuna), **Caminito del Rey** (sells out weeks ahead), and any **Michelin table** (San Sebastián 3★ months ahead).

**🪪 Motorcycle licence note:** 125cc bikes/scooters are fine on a Spanish **B car licence held 3+ years** (or A1). Anything **bigger needs A2/A** — relevant for Mallorca's Tramuntana, the Picos, and the Ronda GS rides, where a 125 undersells the experience.

**💶 Rough bike rental day-rates seen:** 125 scooter €20–40 · mid bike €60–90 · **BMW GS / adventure-touring €90–190/day** (higher in the islands/north, with big deposits).

**☀️ Sun reliability, ranked:** Almería/Cabo de Gata & Costa del Sol & islands (near-guaranteed) → Costa Blanca/Murcia/Cádiz (very reliable) → Lisbon/Algarve (reliable) → **Green North & Douro (a gamble even in July/August — pack a shell).**
